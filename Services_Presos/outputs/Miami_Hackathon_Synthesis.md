## What Every Team Learned (shared themes)

### 1. Claude won most bake-offs
Teams tested Claude, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Rogo, Hebbia, and more. Claude was the main engine in 10 of 12 projects. Big reasons: longer prompts, better with Excel tabs, and no extra cost on top of what we already pay for.

### 2. n8n is the glue
n8n wired up email triggers, file pulls, and auto-sends. It showed up in Valuation, Market Intelligence, Management Hiring, and Board Materials.

### 3. Build beat Buy — almost every time
Teams looked at outside tools. Most found they could build a better fit on Claude in one to two weeks for near zero cost. Vendors we have were already enough. **One clear exception: Data Cubes.** Keye was flat-out faster and cleaner than anything we could build.

### 4. Start small, then grow
Don't wait for a perfect full product. Ship one feature. Add more later. Teams kept repeating this.

### 5. Good prompts beat better models
Fixing the prompt helped more than switching AIs. The prompt *is* the product.

### 6. Show your work
Tools that explain why they picked something get trusted. Partners trust a system that walks them through its thinking.

### 7. Humans still matter at the end
AI is great for the first pass. Not the last mile. Final IC prep, deep LBO models, and real diligence still need a person.

### 8. Data access was the real wall
The models were not the blocker. The blockers were context window limits, LinkedIn walls, and gated sources like PitchBook and Affinity.

### 9. For numbers, split the job
Let the AI pull the data. Let plain code do the math. The LBO team sped up 90x by doing this.

### 10. Templates drive adoption
Same format every time = people actually use it.

## For Leadership — Three Takeaways

### 1. The savings are real and stack across the whole deal
Origination, diligence, IC, portfolio tracking, hiring, valuation, board prep — every stage got faster. If even half the tools roll out broadly, the Services team gets back days per person per month.

### 2. We don't need a big new budget
Most tools were built on stuff we already pay for — Claude, Azure OpenAI, n8n. Build cost was days of team time, not cash.

### 3. The next step is rollout, not more building
Clear next moves from the teams themselves:
- **Keye** — run a 3-month beta for Data Cubes across all deal teams
- **Investment Theme Scout** — roll out as the default starting point for new sector screens
- **Board Materials Prep** — deploy across every PortCo board pack
- **Market Intelligence** — onboard all 30 Services members with tailored feeds
- **Management Hiring stack** — push to PortCos to drive value creation plans
- **Quarterly Valuation** — expand with PRV team across all Services PortCos
